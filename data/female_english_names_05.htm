  3. CHARITY: English name derived from the vocabulary word charity, from Latin caritas, from carus, meaning "dear." It is one of the virtue names that were popular with the Puritans; some others are Chastity, Faith, Honor, Hope, and Prudence.

  13. CHASTITY: English name meaning "purity." It is one of the virtue names that were popular with the Puritans; some others are Charity, Faith, Honor, Hope, and Prudence. 
  14. CHELLE: English short form of French Michelle, meaning "who is like God?"
  15. CHELSEA: English name derived from the name of a district in London, meaning "landing place" or "landing port."

  25. CHERISH: English name derived from the vocabulary word cherish, meaning "to cherish; to hold dear."
  26. CHEROKEE: Unisex name derived from the English pronunciation of Tsa-la-gi, a name that the Native American Cherokee people used to refer to themselves, meaning "principal people."

  32. CHINA: English name derived from the country name which probably got its name from the Qin dynasty. The Chinese people call their country Zhongguo which translates to "middle kingdom." Compare with another form of China.
  33. CHITA: Middle English name meaning "kitten." Compare with other forms of Chita.
  34. CHLOE: Latin form of Greek Chloē, meaning "green shoot." In mythology, this is a surname of the goddess Demeter. In the New Testament bible, this name is mentioned by Paul in 1 Corinthians 1:11.
  35. CHLORIS: Latin form of Greek Khloris, meaning "green buds." In mythology, this is the name of a goddess of flowers and vegetation.

  41. CHRISTABEL: Variant spelling of English Christabelle, meaning "believer" or "follower of Christ" and Belle, "beautiful."

  75. CLAIRENE: Pet form of French Claire, meaning "clear, bright."
  76. CLARA: Feminine form of Latin Clarus, meaning "clear, bright." In use by the English and Italians.
  77. CLARABELLA: English compound name composed of Latin Clara "clear, bright" and Bella "beautiful," hence "bright beauty."
  78. CLARABELLE: Variant spelling of English Clarabella, meaning "bright beauty."
  79. CLARAMAE: English compound name composed of Latin Clara, "clear; bright," and English Mae "pearl" or "obstinate, rebellious."

  88. CLAUDETTE: Diminutive form of French Claudia, meaning "little lame one."

  97. CLOVER: Old English flower name, meaning simply "clover."
  98. COCO: Originally from the Italian occupational name for a "cook," it is now in continental use as a pet form of longer names beginning with Co-.
  99. CODIE: Variant spelling of English unisex Cody, meaning "helper."
  100. CODY: Irish surname transferred to unisex forename use, from a variant spelling of the surname Cuddihy (also spelled Cuddy), an Anglicized form of Gaelic Ó Cuidighthigh "descendant of Cuidightheach," hence "helper."
  101. COLEEN: Variant spelling of English Colleen, meaning "girl."

  107. COLLYN: Variant spelling of English Colleen, meaning "girl."
  108. COLUMBINE: English name derived from the plant name columbine, from Late Latin columbina, meaning "verbina" or "dovelike," so-called because when inverted the flower resembles a cluster of doves. Compare with another form of Columbine.

  116. CORALIE: Pet form of English Coral, meaning "coral" or "small pebble."
  117. CORDELIA: English name which may be an elaborated form of the Latin word cor, meaning "heart." This is the name of a legendary queen of the Britons. It is also the name of a moon of Uranus and an asteroid, both of which were named after a Shakespeare character who also bore this name.
  118. CORIANDER: English name derived from the spice name coriander (also commonly called Cilantro), from Latin coriandrum, from Greek corys ("bedbug") with the added element -ander "resembling" (referring to the smell of the spice which is similar to the odor of bedbugs), hence "smells like a bedbug."
  119. CORLISS: English surname transferred to forename use, from a byname derived from Old English carleas, composed of the elements caru "care; grief" and leas "without," hence "carefree."
  120. CORNELIA: Feminine form of Roman Latin Cornelius, meaning "of a horn." 
  121. CORONA: English name derived from the vocabulary word, Latin corona, meaning "crown, garland, wreath." The outer atmosphere of a star is called a corona.
  122. CORRINA: English variant spelling of Latin Corinna, meaning "maiden."
  123. CORRINE: English variant spelling of French Corinne, meaning "maiden."
  124. CORTNEY: Variant spelling of English unisex Courtney, meaning "short nose."
  125. CORYNN: English variant spelling of French Corinne, meaning "maiden."
  126. COURTNEY: English surname transferred to unisex forename use, from the French baronial name Courtenay, from the nickname court nez, meaning "short nose." 
  127. CRESSIDA: From the English literary name found in various retellings of the Trojan War, based on the Greek name Khryseis, from khrysos, meaning "gold."
  128. CRISTAL: Variant spelling of English Crystal, meaning "crystal, ice."
  129. CRISTEN: English variant spelling of Scandinavian Kristin, meaning "believer" or "follower of Christ."
  130. CRISTINA: Variant spelling of Latin Christina, meaning "believer" or "follower of Christ."
  131. CRYSTAL: English name derived from the gem name, from Greek krystallos, meaning "ice."
  132. CYAN: English name derived from the vocabulary word, from Greek kyanos, meaning "dark blue" and "lapis lazuli." The color cyan is also sometimes called blue-green, electric blue, and turquoise. 
  133. CYBILL: English variant spelling of Greek Sybil, meaning "prophetess." 
  134. CYMONE: English variant spelling of French Simone, meaning "hearkening."
  135. CYNDI: Variant spelling of English Cindy, meaning "woman from Kynthos." 
  136. CYNTHIA: Latin form of Greek Kynthia, meaning "woman from Kynthos." In mythology, this was another name for Artemis.
  137. CYRILLA: Feminine form of English Cyril, meaning "lord."
  138. DACEY: Irish surname transferred to unisex forename use, from an Anglicized form of Gaelic Déiseach which was originally a name for a member of the Déise, "a tenant, a vassal," a word tracing back to Indo-European *dem-s ("house"). 
  139. DAFFODIL: English name derived from the flower name, from Latin asphodelus, from Greek asphodelos, meaning "asphodel flower." In Greek mythology, it is described as a pale yellow deathless kind of lily flower that overspreads the plains of Hades and is the favorite food of the dead. 
  140. DAHLIA: English name derived from the flower name, from the surname of Swedish botanist Anders Dahl, meaning "valley," hence "dahlia flower" or "valley flower."
  141. DAISY: English name derived from the flower name, from Old English dægeseage, "day's eye." 
  142. DAKOTA: English unisex name derived from the name that the Sioux people call themselves, literally meaning "allies."
  143. DALE: English surname transferred to unisex forename use, meaning "dale, valley."
  144. DALIA: Variant spelling of English Dahlia, meaning "dahlia flower" or "valley flower."
  145. DALILAH: Variant spelling of English Delilah, meaning "delicate, weak." 
  146. DALINDA: Variant spelling of English Delinda, meaning "noble serpent." 
  147. DARINA: Anglicized form of Irish Gaelic Dáiríne, meaning "fertile, fruitful."
  148. DALLAS: English surname transferred to unisex forename use, derived from the Scottish place name Dalfhas, composed of the Gaelic elements dail "meadow" and fhas "stance," hence "meadow stance," i.e. a meadow used by cattle droves as an overnight dwelling.
  149. DALYA: Variant spelling of English Dahlia, meaning "dahlia flower."
  150. DAMIA: Feminine form of English Damian, meaning "to tame, to subdue" and euphemistically "to kill."
